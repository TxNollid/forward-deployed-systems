# Skill: Escalation

## When to Use

When you hit a blocker that you can't resolve at your current level of access or authority.

## Common Blockers

| Blocker | Typical Cause | Escalation Path |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Data access denied | Security/compliance concern | → IT Security → CIO → Executive Sponsor |
| Stakeholder unresponsive | Low priority for them | → Day-to-day POC → Their manager → Executive Sponsor |
| Scope creep | Client wants more than SOW | → POC → Your management → Contract amendment |
| Technical infrastructure | Can't get environment/tools | → IT → CIO → Executive Sponsor |
| Political resistance | Someone is blocking the initiative | → Understand why → POC → Executive Sponsor |
| Data quality insufficient | Can't build what was promised | → Reframe scope → POC → Executive Sponsor |
| Timeline slipping | Dependencies not met by client | → Document impact → POC → Joint steering meeting |

## Escalation Process

### 1. Document

Before escalating, write it down:
- What's blocked
- When it was first identified
- What you've tried to resolve it
- Impact on timeline/deliverables if not resolved
- Proposed resolution

### 2. Escalate to the Right Level

Don't skip levels. Try:
1. Direct conversation with the person who can unblock
2. Your day-to-day POC
3. The POC's manager
4. Executive sponsor (last resort for blockers, not first)

### 3. Frame Constructively

Bad: "IT won't give us access and they're blocking the project."
Good: "We need access to [specific system] for [specific reason]. We've requested through [channel] on [date]. The impact of the delay is [X]. Can you help connect us with the right person to expedite?"

### 4. Follow Up

- After escalation, confirm resolution in writing
- Update your project plan with the new timeline
- Thank the people who helped unblock

## When to Escalate Early

- Safety concerns — always, immediately
- Compliance/regulatory risks — always, immediately
- Fundamental scope mismatch — before you build the wrong thing
- Client team not allocated as promised — within the first week
