# Skill: Stakeholder Mapping

## When to Use

First week of any engagement. Update continuously.

## Process

### 1. Identify

Start with the domain's `roles.md` to know who you expect to find. Then map the actual org:

- Who signed the contract / SOW?
- Who is your day-to-day point of contact?
- Who controls budget?
- Who controls data access?
- Who controls IT/infrastructure?
- Who are the end users of what you'll build?

### 2. Classify

For each stakeholder, assess:

| Dimension | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| **Influence** | How much can they accelerate or block you? (High / Medium / Low) |
| **Interest** | How engaged are they in this initiative? (High / Medium / Low) |
| **Stance** | Champion / Supportive / Neutral / Skeptical / Opposed |
| **Information** | What do they know that you need? |

### 3. Strategy

| Quadrant | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| High influence, High interest | **Manage closely.** Regular updates, involve in decisions, make them look good. |
| High influence, Low interest | **Keep satisfied.** Don't overwhelm with detail, but ensure no surprises. |
| Low influence, High interest | **Keep informed.** Good source of ground truth, potential future champions. |
| Low influence, Low interest | **Monitor.** Light touch, don't invest disproportionate time. |

### 4. Document

```markdown
## [Name], [Title]
- **Influence:** High / Medium / Low
- **Interest:** High / Medium / Low
- **Stance:** Champion / Supportive / Neutral / Skeptical / Opposed
- **Motivation:** What do they care about? What's their success metric?
- **Concern:** What are they worried about?
- **Action:** How will you engage them?
- **Cadence:** How often?
```

Store in `memory/` for the engagement.

## Tips

- The org chart is not the influence map. Find the informal power.
- Skeptics aren't enemies — they're often the most valuable allies once won over.
- Ask each stakeholder: "Who else should I talk to?" — this reveals the real network.
- Update your map after every significant meeting.
