# Skill: Change Management

## When to Use

Throughout the engagement, but especially during enablement (Phase 5). If you're building something people won't use, you've failed.

## Core Principle

Technology adoption is a people problem, not a technology problem. The AI can be perfect and still fail if the humans reject it.

## Common Resistance Patterns

| Pattern | What They Say | What They Mean | Response |
|---------|--------------|----------------|----------|
| **Job threat** | "So this replaces us?" | Fear of obsolescence | Show how it augments, not replaces. Quantify time saved on tedious work. |
| **Trust deficit** | "I don't trust the AI" | Burned before by bad tools | Start with transparent recommendations, not black-box automation. Let them override. |
| **Workflow disruption** | "That's not how we do it" | Comfort with current process | Integrate into existing tools/workflows. Don't ask them to learn a new system. |
| **Data skepticism** | "The data isn't good enough" | (They're probably right) | Acknowledge data gaps. Show the model's limitations honestly. |
| **Not-invented-here** | "We tried something like this" | Previous initiative failed | Learn what failed and why. Differentiate your approach explicitly. |

## Adoption Strategy

### 1. Find Champions Early

- Identify 2-3 people who are genuinely excited (not just told to be)
- Give them early access and incorporate their feedback
- Let them present results to peers — peer endorsement > vendor claims

### 2. Start Small, Win Big

- Pick one team, one workflow, one measurable outcome
- Get that working before scaling
- Use the first win as proof for the next expansion

### 3. Make It Easy

- Zero or minimal additional steps in their existing workflow
- If they need to log into a new system, adoption will be low
- Push output to where they already work (email, Teams, existing dashboard)

### 4. Show the Before/After

- Quantify the old way: how long it took, how many errors, how many steps
- Quantify the new way with the same metrics
- Let the numbers speak — don't oversell

### 5. Plan for the Skeptics

- Don't ignore or dismiss skeptics — engage them directly
- Ask what would convince them. Then do that.
- If they have a valid concern, fix it. If they're wrong, show them data.

## Anti-Patterns

- Mandating use without explaining value
- Over-training before anyone has tried the tool
- Declaring victory after go-live instead of monitoring adoption
- Ignoring the informal leaders in favor of official sponsors
